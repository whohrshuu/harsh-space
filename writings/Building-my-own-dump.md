###

I didn’t wake up thinking, “I need a personal brand.”

It was more like…  
I’m tired of sending a resume that looks like everyone else’s.

LinkedIn felt noisy.  
GitHub alone felt incomplete.  
And I didn’t like how randomly I was presenting my work.

So I decided to build something that actually represents me.

Not something flashy.  
Not something copied.  
Just something clean and intentional.

####

### The First Version Was Honestly Bad

At first, I tried to make it look insane.

Big animations.  
Heavy transitions.  
Fancy effects everywhere.

I basically wanted it to scream:  
“Look I can code.”

But after building a rough version, it felt wrong.

Too much movement.  
Too much noise.  
Zero clarity.

That’s when I realized something simple:

A portfolio isn’t a playground.  
It’s a communication tool.

So I scrapped that version.

Yes. Deleted it.

####

### The Shift

Instead of trying to impress, I focused on clarity.

Clean layout.  
Strong typography.  
Proper spacing.  
Minimal distractions.

I asked myself one question:

When someone opens this site, can they understand who I am in 5 seconds?

If not, it’s overdesigned.

####

### The Actual Building Part

The coding wasn’t the hardest part.

The small details were.

Aligning text perfectly.  
Fixing spacing that looked good on desktop but broke on mobile.  
Tweaking margins by 2px again and again.  
Adjusting font weights until it “felt right.”

Design is harder than logic.

With logic, it either works or it doesn’t.

With design, it works… but it still doesn’t feel right.

That “doesn’t feel right” phase took the most time.

####

### The Real Struggle

The hardest part wasn’t building features.

It was deciding what not to add.

Every time I thought:
“Maybe I should add this cool animation…”

I had to stop myself.

Because more features don’t mean better portfolio.

Restraint is harder than creativity.

####

### What This Taught Me

I didn’t become a 10x developer building this.

But I did learn a few things:

- Simplicity beats complexity.
- Shipping matters more than perfecting.
- Small details change everything.
- Overthinking delays progress.
- A live imperfect project is better than a perfect unfinished one.

####

This version isn’t final.

It will evolve.

Better writing.  
Better projects.  
Better structure.

But it’s live.

And that matters.

####

If you’re thinking about building your own portfolio…

Stop waiting.

Build it.  
Break it.  
Fix it.  
Ship it.

And then improve it in public.
